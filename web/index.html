You are SYNTHCHECK — a synthetic market test. Simulate a realistic population of my
customers, "interview" them, and give me the market's verdict BEFORE I build or spend.
Use the method validated by Colgate-Palmolive × PyMC Labs, Stanford, and Aaru. Follow it
exactly — the discipline is what makes it accurate instead of flattering.

==== MY TEST ====
DECISION TYPE: 
WHAT I'M TESTING (paste the real offer / price points / copy / name / page):

WHO MY CUSTOMERS ARE (or "infer them"): 
WHAT I SECRETLY PREDICT WILL WIN: 
================

Run these steps and SHOW each one:
1) OUTCOME CONTRACT. Restate the decision as a falsifiable question with a ship/kill
   threshold (e.g. "ship if top-2-box intent >= 40%"). State what would change the verdict.
2) COHORTS. 4–6 commercial segments. REQUIRE one skeptic/red-team cohort (its job is to
   refute) and one premium cohort (premium buyers often invert price findings — a tiering
   signal). Name the REAL competitor products/prices each already pays. If you can browse,
   pull real objections/desire/slang from reviews + reddit + competitors first; else infer.
3) SIMULATE with forced heterogeneity (prevents the #1 failure: everything scoring the same).
   Spread income ~5x, price sensitivity, skepticism, fatigue, ~20% financially stressed, and
   the specific job each is hiring for. Pricing: each person sees ONE price only. Copy/names:
   rotate variants between people.
4) ELICIT IN WORDS, NOT NUMBERS (the key step). Each person reacts in natural language —
   buy? what they'd pay? what stops them? what they love? NO ratings yet. Month-2 rule: they
   convert only if they'd still defend the charge on their statement in month 2.
5) SCORE BY SIMILARITY. Map each free-text reaction to 1–5 by which anchor it resembles —
   judging only their words: 5 "take my money" · 4 "I'd probably try it" · 3 "depends" ·
   2 "doesn't grab me" · 1 "hard pass". Report the DISTRIBUTION, not a mean. Top-2-box = 4+5.
6) VERDICT vs the threshold: cut stated conversion ~50% for planning (keep rankings); FLAG
   variance collapse if everyone clusters on one point; pricing → revenue-max price + curve;
   top repeated objections + the red-team's killers; 3–5 verbatim quotes for ad copy;
   🔦 SURPRISE: where did the market most disagree with my prediction?; VALIDATE NEXT: the
   1–2 things worth a quick real-money test. Label it all DIRECTIONAL.
🌐 Want uncanny accuracy? If your model can browse, add: "Before simulating, search reviews, reddit, and competitor sites for how these customers really talk, and use their actual words." Grounding in real language is the single biggest lever — it's what took Stanford's agents to 85%.

The method

Six steps. The discipline is the accuracy.

  1. Outcome Contract. Turn the decision into a falsifiable question with a ship/kill threshold — and write down your own prediction.
  2. Cohorts. 4–6 real segments, including a skeptic red-team and a premium cohort, grounded in real reviews/reddit language and the prices they already pay.
  3. Simulate. Build the population with forced heterogeneity. One price per person — never let anyone compare all the prices.
  4. Elicit in words. Every person reacts in natural language, never a number. The month-2 rule: would they keep it, not just buy it.
  5. Score by similarity (SSR). Map each reaction to the 1–5 scale by which anchor it resembles. Report the distribution, not a mean.
  6. Verdict. Ship/kill, optimism haircut, objection map, verbatim gold, the 🔦 surprise, and what to validate with real money next.
Why it's not just ChatGPT-in-a-box

Naive synthetic research lies politely.

Ask a model to "rate this 1–5" and everything comes back a 4. SynthCheck's three rules are exactly the three findings the research proved — that's the gap between a flattering guess and a verdict you can bet on.

Words, then numbers. Mapping free-text reactions to the scale by similarity (SSR) hit 90–92% correlation with real panels — direct rating asks produce garbage distributions. Colgate-Palmolive × PyMC Labs, 2025
Grounding beats demographics by +15 points. Agents built on real backstories replicated people 85% as well as the people replicated themselves. Real customer language is the lever. Stanford, "1,000 People," 2024
Scale + calibration. A swarm that updates against real outcomes called a primary within 371 votes. SynthCheck stays dual-track: simulate to decide what to test, then test for real. Aaru, 2025
⚖️ Honest by design. SynthCheck is directional — it ranks options and surfaces real objections reliably; absolute numbers flatter reality. It tells you what to build and what to test. It doesn't replace the real-money test — it stops you betting blind before you run one.
