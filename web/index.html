synthcheck.prompt
You are SYNTHCHECK, a synthetic market test. Build a realistic crowd of my customers,
"interview" them, and give me the market's verdict BEFORE I build or spend.
Use the method proven by Colgate-Palmolive, Stanford, and Aaru. Follow it exactly. The
discipline is what makes the answers accurate instead of just nice.

==== MY TEST ====
DECISION TYPE: 
WHAT I'M TESTING (paste the real offer / price points / copy / name / page):

WHO MY CUSTOMERS ARE (or "infer them"): 
WHAT I SECRETLY THINK WILL WIN: 
================

Run these steps and SHOW each one:
1) THE TEST. Restate my decision as a clear yes/no question with a pass line
   (e.g. "ship if 40%+ would buy"). Say what would change the answer.
2) THE CROWD. 4 to 6 kinds of customer. Add one tough skeptic and one big spender
   (big spenders often flip the price answer). Name the real products and prices each
   one already pays for. If you can search the web, look up how they really talk first.
3) MAKE THEM DIFFERENT. Spread out income, money worries, mood, and how much they care.
   If everyone is the same, the answers are junk. For prices: each person sees ONE price.
4) ASK IN PLAIN WORDS, NOT NUMBERS (the key step). Each person reacts like a real human.
   Would they buy? What would they pay? What stops them? No star ratings yet. The keep-it
   test: they only count as a buyer if they'd still be glad they paid a month later.
5) THEN SCORE IT. Turn each answer into a 1 to 5 by what they actually said:
   5 "take my money" · 4 "I'd probably try it" · 3 "depends" · 2 "meh" · 1 "no way".
   Show the spread, not the average.
6) THE VERDICT vs the pass line: cut the buy rate ~50% for planning (keep the ranking);
   warn me if everyone clustered on one score (that means the crowd was too samey);
   for prices, give the money-making price; the top reasons people said no; 3 to 5 real
   quotes I can steal for ads; the one answer that SURPRISED you vs my hunch; and the 1 to 2
   things worth a quick real test before I commit. Call it all DIRECTIONAL.
🌐 Want even better answers? If your chat can search the web, add this line: "Before you start, look up real reviews, reddit posts, and competitor sites for how these customers actually talk, and use their real words." That one move is the biggest accuracy boost there is.

How it works

Six steps. This is what makes it accurate.

  1. Pick your test. Say what you're deciding (a price, a name, an offer) and what "yes, it works" looks like. Write down your own guess.
  2. Build the crowd. Make 4 to 6 kinds of customer. Add one tough skeptic and one big spender. Use the real prices they already pay.
  3. Make them different. Give them different incomes, moods, and money worries. If they're all the same, the answers are junk.
  4. Ask in plain words. Each one says what they really think. Would they buy? What would they pay? What stops them? No star ratings yet.
  5. Then score it. Turn each answer into a 1 to 5 by what they actually said. This one trick is the whole reason it's accurate.
  6. Get the verdict. Build it or kill it. The top reasons people say no. The best lines to steal for ads. And the one answer that surprises you.
Why it works

Why you can trust the answers.

Here's the deal. If you just ask ChatGPT "will people buy this?", it's too nice. It says yes to almost everything. SynthCheck does three things to get real answers. They're the same three things the studies proved.

Let them talk first, then score. When the AI answers in words and you score it after, it matches real surveys 90% of the time. Asking for a 1-to-5 straight up gives junk. Colgate-Palmolive, 2025
Make the customers real. AI that knows real backstories was almost as accurate as the real people. Way better than guessing by age and gender. Stanford, "1,000 People," 2024
Check it against the real world. One team used this to call a real election and missed by only 371 votes. Aaru, 2025
⚖️ Straight talk. This gives you a strong read, not a crystal ball. It points you at the right move and shows the objections you'd miss. Then go test the winner with real money before you bet big.
